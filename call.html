Call for participants

We would like to inform about the start of the new research project "Genetic Background of Overgrowth Syndromes in Polish and Lithuanian Populations: Basis for Rapid Genetic Testing to Prevent Neoplasiasâ€œ and cordially invite you to participate. The main information about the project is presented below.

This project aims to determine the genetic background of overgrowth syndromes, develop diagnostic algorithms and optimize healthcare strategy to prevent neoplasia in Polish and Lithuanian populations.

Main tasks of the project:

  1. to compose a cohort of patients with overgrowth conditions divided into clinical subgroups, based on the project questionnaire;
  2. to classify the identified genetic variants into known and/or unknown candidate genes related to overgrowth conditions;
  3. to evaluate what effects identified pathogenic variants may have on the phenotype of patients with overgrowth conditions;
  4. to determine the potential structural significance of identified oncogene and tumour suppressor gene mutations;
  5. to establish rapid and cost-effective diagnostic algorithms for overgrowth conditions;
  6. to establish appropriate management strategies for specific overgrowth conditions to prevent neoplasia and to apply personalized treatment plans.

INCLUSION CRITERIA:

Patients who meet one of the criteria below will be enrolled into one of three study groups:

Group 1 - isolated overgrowth: any individual with overgrowth - defined as height, weight and/or head circumference >97 centile above population mean.

Group 2 - probably syndromic overgrowth: any individual with overgrowth - defined as height, weight and/or head circumference >97 centile above population mean together with at least one additional clinical feature such as:

  • any congenital abnormality,
  • psychomotor delay/learning disability,
  • intelectual disability,
  • skeletal abnormality,
  • neurological abnormality,
  • neoplasia.

Group 3 - asymmetric overgrowth: any individual with asymmetric (static or progressive) overgrowth.

TO JOIN THE PROGRAM, PLEASE PROVIDE THE FOLLOWING:

  1. Filled out clinical project questionnaire for the proband (main patient).
  2. Photographs of proband (2 profile views, au face, the whole body, hands, feet and any specific dysmorphic features)..
  3. Blood samples (taken into tubes with EDTA) - from proband (4 ml, if possible), and from both parents (2 x 9 ml)..
  4. Signed consent form for each participant (proband and parents).

    5. RESULTS:

    1. Study results will be sent to the referring clinician.
    2. Genetic counselling will be offered to each patient/family, especially, when cancer risk is high.

    For more information, please email gospl@mf.vu.lt.
